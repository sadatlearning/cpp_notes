### ### `C++ Primer (4th Edition)` by Stanley B. Lippman

* **Overview**:
  A solid, beginner-to-intermediate book for grasping C++ fundamentals and modern practices (pre-C++11).
* **Highlights**:

  * Covers core concepts like classes, inheritance, and templates.
  * Suitable for students and early professionals.
  * Rich with practical examples and exercises.
* **Limitations**:

  * Now outdated for C++11 and beyond.
  * Verbose in some sections.
* **Best For**: Learners new to C++ who want a strong foundation before diving into modern standards.

---

### ### `The C++ Programming Language (4th Edition)` by Bjarne Stroustrup

* **Overview**:
  Authored by the creator of C++, this book offers an authoritative and comprehensive overview of C++11 and earlier features.
* **Highlights**:

  * Extensive coverage of core language and standard libraries.
  * Includes rationale and design philosophy behind C++ features.
* **Limitations**:

  * Dense and encyclopedic‚Äîmore suitable as a reference than a tutorial.
* **Best For**: Experienced C++ programmers or professionals aiming to master C++11.

### ### `C++ Templates: The Complete Guide (1st Edition)`

by David Vandevoorde, Nicolai Josuttis

* **Overview**:
  A foundational book that deeply explores templates in C++98/03.
* **Highlights**:

  * Explains template syntax, instantiation, and specialization.
  * Good conceptual depth for its time.
* **Limitations**:

  * Dated‚Äîdoes not cover C++11 or newer template features.
* **Best For**: Developers exploring how templates worked in classical C++.

---

### ### `C++ Templates: The Complete Guide (2nd Edition)`

by David Vandevoorde, Nicolai Josuttis, Douglas Gregor

* **Overview**:
  A thoroughly revised and modern version covering C++11, C++14, and C++17 template features.
* **Highlights**:

  * Covers variadic templates, constexpr, SFINAE, and template metaprogramming.
  * Real-world examples with in-depth theory.
* **Limitations**:

  * Advanced concepts may challenge beginners.
* **Best For**: Intermediate to advanced developers seeking mastery of C++ template mechanics in modern C++.

### ### `The Standard Template Library (STL)`

* **1st Edition (1998)**: Early take on STL usage and internals in the C++98 standard.
