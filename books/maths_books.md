# A curated list of **excellent mathematics books** to build your foundation
  - Especially focusing on **Discrete Mathematics, Logic, and Mathematical Induction**.
  - These books are ideal for self-learners and students who want deep understanding.

---

## üìò **1. Discrete Mathematics**

### ‚úÖ **A Logical Approach to Discrete Math** ‚Äì *by David Gries & Fred B. Schneider*

* **Why it's great:** Teaches Discrete Math using **logic-first** approach.
* Covers: Propositional logic, predicate logic, set theory, proofs, recursion.
* Ideal for: Learners who want to think **formally** and understand how math is verified logically.

> ‚≠ê Recommended if you're serious about learning how logic underpins computer science and math.

---

### ‚úÖ **Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications** ‚Äì *by Kenneth Rosen*

* **Why it's great:** Widely used in universities.
* Covers: Logic, proofs, sets, functions, relations, algorithms, counting, graphs, trees.
* Friendly and **broad coverage** of topics with applications.

> Good for general learners and reference throughout your journey.

---

### ‚úÖ **Discrete Mathematics with Applications** ‚Äì *by Susanna Epp*

* **Why it's great:** Strong focus on **proof techniques** and **clear explanations**.
* Emphasizes understanding and writing **correct mathematical arguments**.
* Excellent for beginners who want to improve logical thinking.

---

## üìò **2. Logic and Mathematical Proofs**

### ‚úÖ **How to Prove It: A Structured Approach** ‚Äì *by Daniel J. Velleman*

* **Why it's great:** Step-by-step introduction to **logic**, **proof strategies**, **predicate logic**, and **mathematical reasoning**.
* Builds the foundation needed for all areas of advanced math.
* Clear, friendly, but deep.

> Highly recommended before or alongside any discrete math course.

---

### ‚úÖ **Book of Proof** ‚Äì *by Richard Hammack (Free PDF available online)*

* **Why it's great:** A free, well-written introduction to mathematical proof and logic.
* Covers logic, sets, functions, induction, and more.
* Suitable for self-study.

> [Free download available](https://www.people.vcu.edu/~rhammack/BookOfProof/) from the author's site.

---

## üìò **3. Mathematical Induction**

### ‚úÖ **The Art of Proof: Basic Training for Deeper Mathematics** ‚Äì *by Matthias Beck & Ross Geoghegan*

* Covers all major proof techniques, including **mathematical induction** in detail.
* Teaches you how to think like a mathematician.

---

### ‚úÖ **Principles of Mathematical Analysis** ‚Äì *by Walter Rudin*

(*For advanced readers*)

* Not about discrete math directly, but shows **induction** in real analysis context.
* For those moving from proofs to advanced math.

---

## üîÅ Suggested Study Order

1. **Book of Proof** *(Hammack)* or **How to Prove It** *(Velleman)* ‚Äì for logic and proofs.
2. **A Logical Approach to Discrete Math** *(Gries & Schneider)* or **Epp** ‚Äì for deeper discrete math.
3. **Rosen** ‚Äì as a comprehensive reference or parallel reading.
4. **Mathematical Induction chapters** in those books or **The Art of Proof** ‚Äì for mastering induction.
